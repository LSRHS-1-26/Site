PNGs

The options available for PNGs are very similar to that of GIFs, they are limited to the same amount of simple files. A PNG can often help ou get a smaller file then a GIF but with the same quality. Often times a PNG can actually be better then a GIF quality wise and can handle transparency better then a GIF can. However, a PNG can weigh down a site with its file size more then that of a GIF or a JPG. Another downsaid of using PNGs with transparent backrounds is that the image format is not always supported by the website browser.

JPGs

JPGs are best to you when working iwth photos of grafics with complex effects such as shadows and gradients. JPGs also offer the option to work with a wide range of colors. JPGs allow for you to change the qualtiy of the image, the best solution is usually somewhere in between the lowest and highest quality settings.

GIFs

This is the most complicated image form. GIFs are restricted to 256 colors and should not be use excessivly. The 256 colors make it possible for a very clear and colorized image to be produced and saved.
